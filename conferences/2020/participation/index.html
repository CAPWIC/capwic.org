CAPWIC 2020 Cancelled 😢 - Plan for CAPWIC 2021 at University of Mary Washington

Dear CAPWIC Community,

Today the conference leadership decided to cancel CAPWIC 2020 at Christopher Newport University. We are disappointed we won’t see all of you in person or have our exciting panels, keynotes and fun evening events but we are also convinced this is the responsible decision in light of recent events surrounding Coronavirus (COVID-19).

The most important consideration in our decision is the safety and well-being of our community. The COVID-19 virus is spreading within the United States, cases now exist in Virginia, indications are they will only grow, and if they do so the conference is likely to be cancelled later. By cancelling now, we can refund your registration costs and we hope that you will be able to cancel your travel plans and get refunds.

We encourage sponsors to leave their sponsorship funds for next year's conference, but they can also receive refunds if preferred. We'll be in touch separately to ask your preference and discuss the details.

We will not be able this year to provide a complete online version of the conference, but organizers are looking into what we can provide to support you locally in mentoring one another and encouraging the participation of women in computing. You will be receiving more detailed information from us about that in the days and weeks to come.

In the meantime planning for CAPWIC 2021 at the University of Mary Washington is already underway. Organizers will also be considering how to plan for a variety of online and in-person participation at that celebration. We hope that you can join us then!

Thank you for your support and participation in CAPWIC 2020. The conference has really grown with over 300 registrations this year. Seeing all the submissions and interacting with so many of you has been wonderful. Keep up the good work everyone!

With deep appreciation,
Lynn Lambert and Dee A. B. Weikle
CAPWIC 2020 Co-Chairs

CAPWIC 2020

There are seven tracks to which you can submit your work. Please submit by the January 31, 2020 submission deadline for full consideration.

CAPWIC 2020, the ACM Capital Region Celebration of Women in Computing, will be held at Christopher Newport University, in Newport News, Virginia. The goal of CAPWIC is to bring together female students and allies to promote the recruitment, retention, and progression of women in computing fields. We invite submissions in the area of computing research as well as on efforts to increase diversity in computing in the following formats:

Our call for submissions flyer is available for download here.

Unfortunately, we are unable to sponsor visas for international travel for CAPWIC. Submit now!

Flash Talks

Five-minute presentations with five minutes for questions. Fast-paced talks on preliminary results, work-in progress, or anything fun and creative related to the conference are welcome in this category. All levels of experience are welcome.

Student Posters

Before the conference, presenters create a 24” x 32” poster of research, a class project, or other type of work-in-progress including efforts to diversify computing and are present at the poster during the session to discuss content. Open to all students. Prizes will be awarded.

Student Research Shorts

Research shorts are an opportunity to give a 15-minute presentation with an additional 5 minutes for questions, similar to a talk at a research conference. Work should be technical in nature, investigating new ideas in any area of computer science. The intention is to give presenters a friendly environment in which to practice a future technical talk or work out details in an ongoing project to get constructive feedback. Only an abstract is required, so future publication of the work is not limited.

Technical Workshops

Workshops present a valuable and current technical skill in a hands-on training session. Proposals must include abstract, the intended audience and power/AV equipment/space needs.

Panels

Panels present multiple perspectives on a topic. Proposals should include abstract, a list of presenters including their affiliation and perspective, along with a proposed length of 25 or 50 minutes.

Birds-of-a-Feather

Birds-of-a-feather (BOF) sessions are opportunities for a group of participants with similar concerns to have an informal discussion. BOF sessions will be 30 minutes.

Technical Talks

25 minute technical talk with 5 minutes for questions on a relevant and current technical topic. Priority given to younger faculty and industry partners.
