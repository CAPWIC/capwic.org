April 5-6, 2024

CAPWIC is the ACM Capital Region Celebration of Women in Computing and provides a low-cost, regionally tailored, small conference for women and minorities in computing. The participants include women (students, faculty, and professionals), as well as all supporters of women in computing.

The conference will be hosted by the University of Virginia, on April 5-6, 2024. All levels of experience are welcome.

Dr. Cynthia Bailey

Cynthia Bailey is a Senior Lecturer in the Computer Science Department at Stanford. She has a PhD in high-performance computing from UC San Diego, and her other scholarly specializations include computer science education, tech/AI ethics and social impact, and machine learning/AI algorithms. Her teaching awards include the President's Lloyd W. Dinkelspiel Award for exceptional contributions to undergraduate education at Stanford, a "Top 10 Papers of All Time" award at the 50th anniversary of the ACM SIGCSE technical symposium, and the Stanford Society of Women Engineers' Professor of the Year. Outside of academia, her work experience includes NASA Ames, startups, and expert witness testifying for top intellectual property law firms. She is on leave from Stanford this year to work on AI Policy in the U.S. Senate, as a AAAS AI Policy Fellow.

Beyond Graduation: Finding Your Community as a Professional Woman in Technology

As college students, women who are in technology fields often find themselves in the minority. Luckily, most students are able to access supportive student groups such as SWE, WIC, women in data science, and others. But what happens when you graduate and land your first job working as a women in tech? Many women find themselves at companies that are even more gender- imbalanced than their college programs, often in new cities where they have to build new connections from scratch. Many networking groups for women in technology are hard to find or only offer conferences or meetups once or twice a year. It can be challenging to find a supportive community that offers guidance from women's perspective on professional situations such as salary negotiation, getting recognition for your work, and professional development. And as women progress in their careers, additional challenges emerge, including navigating management tracks, maternity leave policies, and juggling family life. Charlottesville Women in Tech (CWIT) is a non-profit that was created to address this need for connection and support among women who work in technology careers. This panel will feature CWIT board members who will share their stories about feeling alone in new jobs, seeking connections, and creating a strong, thriving group of local women who can network and guide each other through career (and life) challenges. The panel will discuss ways to find women in similar situations, how to create networking opportunities, and how to continue fostering connections throughout your entire career.

Panelists
  • Eileen Krepkovich, Senior Research Scientist at Barron Associates and President of Charlottesville Women in Tech

    Eileen Krepkovich is a senior research scientist at Barron Associates, where she works with wearable devices for medical rehabilitation. She has degrees in biomedical engineering from Case Western Reserve and Northwestern, and has 20 years' experience working in biomedical research. She co-founded Charlottesville Women in Tech in 2014 to help find a network of other local women working in technical fields, and has served as president of the board for the past 10 years.

  • Elaine Cheng, Chief Information Officer at Shentel and Board Member of Charlottesville Women in Tech

    Elaine Cheng is a Senior Vice President and Chief Information Officer for Shentel (Shenandoah Telecommunications). She leads the Information Technology organization, Customer Service, Enterprise Project Management Office (EPMO), and Enterprise Risk Management program. Elaine has over 30 years of experience in diverse business environments across all areas of Information Technology. Prior to joining Shentel, Elaine served as Chief Information Officer and Managing Director of Global Strategic Design for CFA Institute in Charlottesville, Va. Prior to her time at CFA Institute, Elaine held a number of different roles over 16 years with M&T Bank in Buffalo, NY, including Group Vice President, Technology Business Services, Vice President of Retail Operations and Assistant Vice President, Web Product Owner. She received her Bachelor of Arts degree from Vassar College and her Masters of Business Administration from the University of Rochester. More importantly, Elaine is a founding board member of Charlottesville Women in Tech, a non-profit organization which encourages women to join and thrive in technology careers.
